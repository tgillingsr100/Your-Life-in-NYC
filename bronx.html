Congratulations! You Chose The Bronx

Level Unlocked: Birthplace of Hip-Hop

You just entered the borough where hip-hop began. In the 1970s, DJ Kool Herc changed music by focusing on the “break” of songs, creating space for people to dance, rap, and express themselves.

As you move through the Bronx level, you unlock raw talent across generations. Fat Joe and Terror Squad helped represent the borough in the 2000s, showing collaboration, diversity, and Bronx pride.

Next, you unlock a new sound. A Boogie wit da Hoodie brought melodic rap, blending singing and rap into something more emotional and relatable.

Now you reach the modern stage. Ice Spice represents the new generation, using confidence, social media, and Bronx energy to reach a wider audience.

Final Boss: Drill Music

The Bronx drill scene brings aggressive beats, raw lyrics, and real-life experiences. It has a major influence on youth culture, shaping both sound and identity.

You completed the Bronx level. From DJ Kool Herc to today’s artists, the Bronx proves that raw talent still shapes the future of hip-hop.
